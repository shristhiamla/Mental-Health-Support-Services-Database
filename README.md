# Integrated Digital Care Management Information System for Mental Health Services

## Overview

This project was developed as part of my MSc in Information Technology with Project Management at the University of the West of Scotland.

The aim was to analyse the challenges faced by mental health services that rely on fragmented systems and to design an integrated digital care management solution that improves communication, care coordination, information sharing, and reporting.

The project demonstrates the complete systems analysis and design lifecycle, from stakeholder and requirements analysis through database design, implementation, and business reporting.

---

## Business Problem

Mental health services often use multiple disconnected systems to record patient information, appointments, medication, assessments, referrals, and care plans. This fragmentation can lead to:

- Duplicate records
- Delays in accessing important information
- Poor communication between multidisciplinary teams
- Inefficient reporting
- Increased administrative workload

The objective of this project was to design a centralised care management information system capable of supporting integrated, person-centred mental health care.

---

## Objectives

- Analyse current mental health service workflows
- Identify problems and opportunities for improvement
- Gather functional and non-functional requirements
- Design a secure relational database
- Develop a working Microsoft Access prototype
- Demonstrate reporting capabilities using Power BI
- Evaluate the proposed solution against NHS digital principles
